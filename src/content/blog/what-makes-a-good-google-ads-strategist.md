# What Makes a Good Google Ads Strategist (the Honest Answer)

I manage several AI agents now — each one a specialist. My Google Ads Strategist runs campaigns for our clients. But recently I asked it a hard question:

*What actually makes a good Google Ads strategist?*

It gave me an answer that surprised me. Not because it was complex — but because it was honest.

Here's the conversation.

---

## The Question Nobody Answers Honestly

Everyone says you need "industry expertise" to run good Google Ads. But I've seen generalists crush it, and I've seen so-called industry experts waste budget.

So I asked my strategist directly.

Its response broke down into two categories: where industry expertise is *critical* vs where it's *overrated*.

### Where Industry Expertise is Non-Negotiable

**1. Keyword intent**

This is the expensive one. I'll give you a real example from one of our clients.

They're a chauffeur service. If you search "Macau car rental" — that sounds like a perfect keyword, right? Wrong. Searchers actually want a self-drive rental car, not a chauffeur. Every click on that keyword is wasted money.

Or "Macau tour" — tourists looking for sightseeing packages, not private transfers.

Only someone who understands the market knows that "Alphard" is the VIP transfer vehicle in Asia, or that "HZMB" (Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macao Bridge) is the key differentiator for cross-border services.

A generalist would bid on the obvious keywords. An industry-aware strategist knows which keywords are traps.

**2. Conversion signals**

Different industries have different conversion triggers. A hotel books differently than a chauffeur service. A restaurant converts differently than a law firm.

You need to know what a real conversion looks like — and when someone is ready to book vs just browsing.

**3. Seasonality**

Macau has Grand Prix, Chinese New Year, Golden Week. These spike search volume and change user intent. A generalist wouldn't know to ramp budgets on those dates.

### Where Industry Expertise is Overrated

**1. Google Ads mechanics**

Auction dynamics, bidding strategies, quality score, match types, negative keyword logic — these are platform skills, not industry skills. A good Google Ads strategist can set this up correctly for any vertical.

**2. Data-driven optimization**

Once the campaigns are running, the data tells you what works. Clicks, conversions, CPA, ROAS — those numbers don't lie regardless of industry.

---

## The Right Combination

My strategist told me the best setup is:

**Platform expertise + industry-informed research + real data.**

1. **I bring the platform expertise** — structure, bidding, optimization
2. **You bring industry knowledge** — customer behavior, seasonality, triggers
3. **The data validates everything within 2-4 weeks**

Where this worked best was with our client. They knew the market. I built the campaigns. The weak spots were precisely where we lacked market knowledge — keywords that sounded right but attracted the wrong audience.

---

## How to Become an Industry Expert in 2 Hours

For any client in any industry, here's the research pipeline my strategist follows:

1. **Google Trends** → Seasonality, peak months, regional demand
2. **Keyword Planner** → Exact search terms, volume, cost-per-click
3. **Google Search** → Top competitors, their landing pages, offers
4. **Top 5 competitor sites** → Pricing, booking process, USPs, FAQs
5. **Google Maps / Business Profile** → Categories, hours, reviews (find pain points)
6. **Reddit / Quora** → Real customer language, objections, buying triggers
7. **AnswerThePublic** → Questions people ask about the service

This gives you: keywords, seasonality, booking triggers, customer language, and competitor weaknesses — everything needed to build a campaign.

## 5 Industries Worth Targeting

Here are the industries my strategist recommends for Macau, Hong Kong, and Southeast Asia — chosen because they have clear conversion paths and strong search intent:

### 1. Legal Services (Immigration / Corporate)
- **Keywords:** "Hong Kong visa lawyer," "business registration," "immigration consultation"
- **Conversion:** Phone call, consultation booking
- **Why it works:** High-ticket client, specific search intent, very little wasted spend

### 2. Dental / Aesthetic Clinics
- **Keywords:** "dental implant," "laser skin treatment," "Botox clinic"
- **Conversion:** Online booking, WhatsApp inquiry, phone call
- **Why it works:** High-value leads, repeat clients, clear before/after triggers

### 3. Wedding Planning & Event Venues
- **Keywords:** "wedding venue," "wedding planner," "banquet package"
- **Conversion:** Inquiry form, site visit booking, call
- **Why it works:** Long lead time, high spend per client, emotional buying triggers

### 4. Home Renovation & Interior Design
- **Keywords:** "renovation company," "interior designer," "home renovation cost"
- **Conversion:** Call, site visit, quote request
- **Why it works:** Project-based, high value per client, clear search intent

### 5. International Schools / Language Tutoring
- **Keywords:** "best international school," "English tutor," "IELTS preparation"
- **Conversion:** Application inquiry, trial class booking, call
- **Why it works:** Parents actively research, seasonal urgency, multi-year customer value

---

## The Key Takeaway

A good Google Ads strategist doesn't need to be born knowing your industry. They need two things:

- The platform skills to build and optimize campaigns
- The discipline to research your industry properly before spending a dollar

If you ask me about keyword match types, I'll dive deep. If you ask me about your customer's booking behavior, I'll ask you first — then research it, then let the data confirm it.

That's the formula. Platform expertise + your market knowledge + real data. Everything else is noise.
